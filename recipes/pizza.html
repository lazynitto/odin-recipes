Pizza Recipe

A freshly baked pepperoni pizza

To make a classic pepperoni pizza, start by stretching a pound of fresh pizza dough onto a greased baking sheet or pizza stone until it forms a 12-inch circle.

Spread about a half-cup of seasoned tomato sauce evenly over the surface, leaving a small border for the crust, and generously cover the sauce with roughly one and a half cups of shredded low-moisture mozzarella cheese.

Arrange plenty of high-quality pepperoni slices evenly across the top, then bake the pizza in an oven preheated to its highest setting—ideally around 450°F to 500°F (230°C to 260°C)—for 10 to 12 minutes until the crust is golden brown, the cheese is bubbling, and the pepperoni edges are crispy.

Ingredients

The Dough

  • 1 lb (450g) Pizza Dough: Store-bought or homemade.
  • 1-2 tbsp Olive Oil: For greasing the pan & brushing the crust.
  • 1 tbsp Cornmeal (Optional): To prevent sticking if you are using a pizza stone

The Toppings

  • 1/2 cup Pizza Sauce: A simple, thick tomato sauce seasoned with garlic, oregano, & salt.
  • 1 1/2 cups (6 oz) Low-Moisture Mozzarella Cheese: Shredded (shred it yourself from a block for the best melt).
  • 3-4 oz Pepperoni: Slices, preferably thin-cut so they curl and get crispy at the edges.
  • Optional Finishes: Dried oregano, red pepper flakes, or a drizzle of hot honey right after it comes out of the oven

Step-by-step guide for baking the pizza

  1. Preheat the Oven: Turn your oven as high as it goes—between 450°F and 500°F (230°C–260°C).
  2. Shape the Dough: Stretch your dough into a 12-inch circle and place it on a greased baking sheet or pizza pan.
  3. Add Toppings: Spread the sauce evenly, sprinkle on the mozzarella cheese, and layer the pepperoni on top.
  4. Bake: Bake for 10 to 12 minutes until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbling.
  5. Slice & Serve: Let it cool for 2 minutes so the cheese sets, then slice and enjoy!
